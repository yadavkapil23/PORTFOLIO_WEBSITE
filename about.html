About

The engineer behind the builds.

I'm Kapil — a Computer Science undergrad at UPES, Dehradun, who spends most of his time at the intersection of AI/ML, backend engineering, and cloud-ready full-stack development. I like building software that's not just clever in a notebook, but actually ships — APIs that are easy to integrate, models that survive contact with real users, and pipelines that don't break the moment they leave my laptop.

Most of my recent work has been in the AI + applied ML space. I've built a containerized RAG assistant on LangChain, FastAPI, and FAISS that handles 50+ concurrent users, with Wikipedia and LLM fallbacks that boosted answer accuracy by 35%. I've trained a CNN/ResNet-18 underwater acoustic classifier with custom MFCC feature pipelines, and shipped a full Django book platform (NovelNet) integrating Google Books API and Supabase. I care a lot about the unglamorous parts — Dockerfiles, CI/CD on GitHub Actions, Azure deployments, and writing the kind of READMEs I wish other people wrote.

Earlier in 2025 I interned remotely as a Web Developer at Weazy, where I architected a Flask + microservices backend for an e-commerce catalog — automating product image fetching with Selenium and BeautifulSoup, then deploying it on Azure with a CI/CD pipeline. Around the same time I served as a Student Developer with the OPEN Community, contributing to open-source Python and JavaScript projects and running knowledge-sharing sessions on Flutter and Git for 15+ new contributors.

Outside of code, I'm usually reading about generative AI, tinkering with vector databases, or sketching out the next side project on a whiteboard. I'm always open to interesting collaborations — whether that's an AI product idea, an open-source contribution, or just a good engineering conversation.
